Staff Quarters at Fo Tan

The Development


Built Upon the hillside of Fo Tan at Shatin and attracts the scenery of Shatin Town Centre, the development was built by the University and occupied in Autumn 1994. It composed a total of 113 units in 5 Towers with 8 storeys in each Tower.

All 5 Towers of development were used as the staff quarters of the University until end of June, 2000. In response to the introduction of new home financing scheme by the government, two towers (Tower 4 & Tower 5) were returned to the government in July, 2000. At present, Tower 1 to Tower 3 are used as the staff quarters of the University while Tower 4 & Tower 5 are used as government quarters for civil servants.

Carpark

There are a total of 85 car parking spaces (60 covered spaces and 25 open spaces) for use by residents with valid Parking Permit issued by the Property Management Company. All parking spaces owned by the University are marked by yellow border. Residents are advised to observe the carpark regulations and parked in parking spaces marked yellow. Guests parking is also available upon registration at the entrance guard house.

Useful Tips

Door Phone System & Entrance Code
Acces by Residents
  1. Press "R" once.
  2. Key-in 4 digits access code (changed periodically with prior notice).
  3. The door latch will be released if the key-in code is correct.
  4. Press "CLEAR" to try again if in need.
Garbage Disposal
A plastic garbage bin is put at the corridor in-between the rear door of Flat A and Flat C on each floor. Residents are advised to put the domestic garbage in a fastened bag, and then put into the plastic garbage bin to prevent spread of bad smell. Residents who want to dispose their own household furniture or appliances especially during their move-out, are advised to put the disposal items at the refuse collection chamber near Tower 2 by mover or other means. The cleaning staff have no obligation to dispose the household furniture / appliances for the residents.
Precaution Measures
  1. Turn off the electricity, water and gas supply of your flats.
  2. All flower pots, drying items or articles that can be blown away should be brought inside the flat.
  3. Ensure that all the water taps are turned off after use.
  4. Use safe and standard plugs and adapters for your electrical appliances and equipment.
  5. Make sure that all the gas appliances e.g. gas oven, gas heaters are turned off after use.
  6. Do not release the code numbers for entrance doors to outsiders.
Others
Gas heaters are provided in each washroom inside the flat. The maintenance responsibility for the gas heaters belongs to The Hong Kong and China Gas Company Limited. For annual checking of the gas heaters and/or repair/maintenance, you can make appointment with the Gas Company at 2880 6988 directly or contact the Estates Office of HKBU.
When You Move-Out
A preliminary moving-out inspection will be arranged with you at about one week before your actual move-out date to ascertain if any reinstatement works is/are required in your apartment.

Property Manager of the Staff Quarters

A competent Property Management Company, SINO Property Management Co. Ltd (SINO) is appointed to manage the common areas and facilities of Staff Quarters. A team of experience and responsible staff is deployed on-site by SINO to take care the day-to-day management duties.

Scope of Service
  1. To operate a Management Office and a 24 hours security control room.

  2. To provide security, cleaning and landscaping service to the common areas.

  3. To provide repair and maintenance service to the common areas and facilities e.g. swimming pool, lift, fire service system, carpark facility etc.

  4. To handle emergency incident e.g. fire, flooding. lift trap, etc if happened.

All staff are not allowed to work for residents privately for a return nor can they receive instructions which may conflict with their normal duties. Should you have any suggestions/requests in related to the management and maintenance of the Staff Quarters, you can express your opinions by filling and returning the Suggestion Form or contact the Estates Office directly.
